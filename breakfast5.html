French Toast Casserole

Wake up to the smell of this delicious dairy free french toast casserole! Prepped the night before, it’s absolutely perfect for the holidays, brunches or company. Easy to make and packed with blueberries, it bakes up soft underneath and delightfully crisp on top.

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 cup canned light coconut milk, shaken/stirred well
  • 1/3 cup pure maple syrup
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 8 ounces french or sourdough bread, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup fresh blueberries, divided
  1. Grease an 8x8 inch baking dish with nonstick or olive oil spray then set aside
  2. Add the eggs to a medium bowl and whisk until combined. Add in the coconut milk, maple syrup, vanilla extract, almond extract, cinnamon and nutmeg and whisk again until smooth.
  3. Add half of the bread cubes to the prepared baking dish, then top with half of the blueberries. Repeat this process with the remaining half of the bread cubes and blueberries.
  4. Evenly pour the egg mixture over the top, then gently press the bread down into the liquid to ensure that it's completely wet.
  5. Cover the dish tightly with plastic wrap and transfer to the refrigerator. Let sit for at least several hours but ideally overnight (up to 12 hours).
  6. Preheat the oven to 350ºF. Remove the casserole from the refrigerator and discard the plastic wrap.
  7. Bake for about 50 to 60 minutes, until golden brown, puffed in the center and the custard is set. If the top ever starts browning too much, tent loosely with foil.
  8. Let stand for about 10 to 15 minutes, then serve as desired!
